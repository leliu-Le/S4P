In spring, there are no human live in the city of Guangzhou. We are all fish with two legs. We press our finger against the wall, tracing a circle, drawing two dots within it, adding an arc, and we see water dripping down from every stroke we drew. A melting smiley face. The floor is always wet. Some of us tried to mop the floor to dry it up, but it never works. Mopping just rearranges the water droplets. Those tiny droplets gather and form larger ones. The floor never dries, and we have to step on the freshly mopped surface, leaving a trace of dirty footprints. We try to mop and wipe away these footprints, but the floor never dry. We will always leave a new trail of dirty footprints. We walk on tile floor, we think about each step we take. If we take the step too fast, we will slip and fall on the floor that never fully dries out from the last mopping; if we lift our feet too low, we will scrape against the floor, making a sharp high-pitched noise, like the sound of fingernail scratching a blackboard. Our clothes never dry. The laundry washed a week ago still hangs on the balcony. Or maybe the clothes dry a bit, and get soaked again by spring storm. Those clothes stink.
The spring in Guangzhou is teal. It is the color of the never ending rain, of the humidity in the air, of the hazy mist on the window glass with greenery behind. We live in water. We swim in water. We see bubble in the air.
